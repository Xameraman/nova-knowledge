WHAT IS URLSPAY
---------------
Urlspay is a completely free URL shortener that pays you for every click your
links receive. It's a "pay-per-view" shortener — similar to Linkvertise, Adf.ly,
and Shorte.st — where visitors must go through several unlock pages (with timers,
verification steps, and ads) before reaching the final content. The creator earns
money per view: Urlspay claims up to $20 per 1,000 views from global traffic.

Urlspay explicitly advertises itself as "High CPM Url Shortner With Simple Pages"
and operates in 3 steps: create an account, create a short link, and share it.
As of its last reported stats, the platform has served over 4.5 million total
clicks, hosts over 939,000 shortened URLs, and has around 14,000 registered users.

Q: What is Urlspay?
A: Urlspay is a free URL shortener that pays creators when people click

before reaching the final destination. It is similar to Linkvertise, Adf.ly,
and Shorte.st but has its own unique unlock flow.

HOW URLSPAY WORKS (FOR VISITORS)
--------------------------------
When you click a Urlspay link, you are taken to an intermediate page
(not the final content). You must complete several rounds of unlocking
before being redirected to the actual file or website.

Each unlock round typically involves:
- Waiting for a countdown timer (usually around 20 seconds)
- Scrolling to the bottom of the page
- Clicking "Verify" or a similar button
- Waiting again for the button to change
- Clicking "Continue" or "Unlock Link"

This process repeats 3 to 5 times before you reach the final link.

Q: How many times do I have to unlock?
A: Usually 3 to 5 rounds. Each round follows the same pattern:
wait, scroll, verify, continue. The exact number varies by link.

Q: Why does Urlspay make me wait so long?
A: The waiting time and repeated steps are designed to show you
advertisements. The creator earns money from these ad views. This is
how the entire Urlspay business model works. It's the "High CPM" model
— more ad views = higher earnings for the link creator.

Q: Does Urlspay use cryptocurrency?
A: Scamadviser detected cryptocurrency-related services on the site, which
they consider high-risk. If you encounter crypto-related prompts or offers
during the unlock process, avoid engaging with them.


STEP-BY-STEP GUIDE TO UNLOCK A URLSPAY LINK
-------------------------------------------

Step 1 — Open the Link Properly
If you opened the link inside an app like YouTube, Discord, or
Instagram, the in-app browser may cause problems:
- Tap the three dots (⋮) menu in the top right
- Select "Open in Chrome" or "Open in Browser"
- If on iPhone, tap the Safari icon to open externally

Always use Chrome or Safari — in-app browsers can break the unlock
process.

Q: The link won't open in the in-app browser. What do I do?
A: Tap the three dots menu and choose "Open in Chrome" or "Open in
external browser". In-app browsers (YouTube, Discord, Instagram) often
block the scripts needed for the unlock flow.

Q: Can I use Firefox or another browser?
A: Yes, any modern browser works. Chrome is recommended because it's
the most compatible with these types of link services. Safari on
iPhone also works fine.


Step 2 — Initial Verification
When you first land on the Urlspay page:
- Wait a few seconds for the page to fully load
- A "Continue to Content" or similar button will appear
- Press it after verification completes

Sometimes this step shows a brief "verifying your browser" message.
Just wait — it usually takes 3–5 seconds.

Q: The page just shows a blank screen or keeps loading forever.
A: Try refreshing the page. If that doesn't work, close the tab and
open the link again in Chrome. Also disable any ad blockers
temporarily — they can interfere with the verification.


Step 3 — First Unlock
- Wait about 20 seconds (a countdown timer may be visible)
- Tap the "Unlock Link" button when it becomes active
- Scroll all the way to the bottom of the page
- Press the "Verify" button
- Wait a moment while the button processes
- The button will change to "Continue" — press it

Important: Always scroll to the very bottom. Some buttons are hidden
below the visible area and you may miss them if you don't scroll.

Q: I scrolled down but can't find the Verify button.
A: Scroll slowly and look carefully near the bottom of the page.
Sometimes the button is small or placed after a block of ads. If you
still can't find it, try zooming out or rotating your phone to
landscape mode.

Q: The timer keeps resetting.
A: This can happen if you switch tabs or your phone screen turns off.
Stay on the page until the timer completes. Don't minimize the
browser or switch to another app during the countdown.


Step 4 — Repeat the Unlock Process
You will go through this cycle multiple times (usually 3 to 5 rounds).
Each round follows the exact same pattern:

1. Wait approximately 20 seconds
2. Tap "Unlock Link"
3. Scroll all the way to the bottom
4. Press "Verify"
5. Wait for verification to complete
6. Press "Continue" when it appears

The page may look slightly different each round, but the steps are
always the same. Don't get discouraged — this is normal for Urlspay.

Q: I've done this 5 times and it's still not done. Am I stuck?
A: Some links require up to 7 rounds. If it goes beyond 7 rounds
without reaching the final page, the link may be broken or the system
may be glitching. Try refreshing the page and starting over, or
contact the person who shared the link for a direct alternative.

Q: The button says something different like "Get Link" or "Access
Content". Is that normal?
A: Yes. The exact button text varies. Common variations include:
"Unlock Link", "Get Link", "Continue to Content", "Access Now",
"Get Access", "Download". All of them serve the same purpose.


Step 5 — Final Step
After the last unlock round:
- Press "Unlock Link" one more time
- Scroll down to the bottom
- Press "Continue" or "Get Link"
- You will be redirected to the actual file, download page, or
destination website

If the final destination is a file download, your browser may ask
where to save it. If it's another website, it will open normally.

Q: I reached the final page but it's just another ad site.
A: Some Urlspay links are set up by malicious users who stack multiple
redirect services. If you land on another unlock page (like
Linkvertise), the creator chained multiple services together. MIA
never shares links like this — always request a direct link instead.

IMPORTANT NOTES AND WARNINGS
----------------------------

Ads and Popups
During the unlock process, popup ads or new tabs may open:
- DO NOT click on any ads
- DO NOT download anything from popups
- Close popup tabs immediately
- Never enter personal information, email, or phone number

Popups are part of how Urlspay generates revenue for the link creator.
They are annoying but can be safely closed.

Q: A popup says my phone is infected with a virus. Is it real?
A: No. This is a fake scare tactic ad. Do not click anything on it.
Close the tab immediately. These ads try to trick you into downloading
malicious software. Your phone is not infected.

Q: The page asked me to install a Chrome extension. Should I?
A: No! Never install browser extensions or apps from link shortener
pages. These are usually adware, spyware, or malware. Close the tab.

If Stuck or Progress Resets
- Refresh the page by swiping down or pressing the reload button
- Make sure you're using Chrome or an external browser, not an
in-app browser
- If your screen times out or you switch tabs, the timer may reset
- Close all tabs and start fresh if needed

Q: My progress keeps resetting after I complete a round. Why?
A: This usually happens because of:
- Using an in-app browser (always switch to Chrome/Safari)
- Having cookies blocked (Urlspay uses cookies to track progress)
- Using a VPN or proxy (some are blocked)
- Having an ad blocker enabled (try disabling it temporarily)

Q: Can I use an ad blocker with Urlspay?
A: Ad blockers can break the unlock flow because Urlspay relies on
showing ads. If you're stuck, try temporarily disabling your ad
blocker for the unlock process, then re-enable it afterwards.

Scam & Safety Warnings
Urlspay has a LOW TRUST SCORE (47/100 on Scamadviser). Documented issues:
- Some users report being charged without authorization after signing up
- Trustindex reviewers call it a "scam" with accounts cancelled randomly
- Trustpilot rating: "Poor" (1.9/5) — complaints about nonexistent support
- The registrar (NameCheap) hosts many low-reputation sites
- Cryptocurrency services on the site are flagged as HIGH RISK
- Domain is relatively new (registered July 2024)
RECOMMENDATION: Never pay Urlspay for any upgrade or premium service.
If you want to use it as a creator, accept that payouts may never arrive.
For visitors: never enter payment info or personal details.

Using Chrome (Recommended)
Always use Chrome or your device's default browser — not an in-app
browser. In-app browsers found in apps like YouTube, Discord, TikTok,
Instagram, and Facebook often break the scripts that run the countdown
timers and verification buttons.

Desktop vs Mobile
The unlock process works on both desktop and mobile. On desktop:
- You may see more ads and popup windows
- A popup blocker browser extension is recommended
- The "scroll to bottom" step is easier since you can see more of
the page at once

On mobile:
- Popup redirects are harder to manage
- Stay on the same tab — switching apps resets the timer
- Rotate to landscape if buttons are cut off
