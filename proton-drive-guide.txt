## 1. What Is Proton Drive?
Proton Drive is an end‑to‑end encrypted cloud storage service developed by Proton AG, the Swiss company behind Proton Mail, Proton VPN, Proton Calendar, and Proton Pass. Founded in 2014 by scientists who met at CERN, Proton is headquartered in Geneva, Switzerland. The company’s products are built on a foundation of privacy, security, and open‑source transparency.

Proton Drive was launched in 2022 as the cloud storage component of the Proton ecosystem. Unlike many other file hosts, Proton Drive encrypts all files, file names, and metadata on the user’s device before they ever reach Proton’s servers. This means Proton cannot read your files — even if compelled by a legal order. Data is stored in secure data centers in Switzerland and Germany, protected by the world’s strongest privacy laws.

## 2. How Proton Drive Encryption Works
Proton Drive’s encryption is unique among file hosts. Here’s what happens behind the scenes when you download a shared file:

- File encryption: The creator’s files are encrypted on their device before upload. Proton Drive uses AES‑256 encryption for file content and RSA for key exchange.
- Secure sharing: When a creator shares a file, Proton Drive generates an encrypted share link. The file remains encrypted on Proton’s servers — Proton never has the decryption key.
- Your download: When you open a Proton Drive link, your browser downloads the encrypted file data. Your browser then uses cryptography (via the Web Crypto API) to decrypt the file locally, on your device, before saving.
- The decryption delay: Because the file must be decrypted in your browser, downloads don’t start instantly. Your browser must first verify the file signature, decrypt the data, and then assemble the final file. This can take a few seconds for small files, and longer for large files, especially on mobile devices with limited processing power or memory.

Important practical takeaway: If a Proton Drive download seems to "pause" or "not start," it’s usually because your browser is still decrypting the file. Wait — almost always, the download will begin after the decryption finishes.

## 6. Download Process (Step‑by‑Step)
Step 1: Open the Proton Drive link. The URL typically contains “drive.proton.me” or “proton.drive”.

Step 2: If prompted for a password, enter it and submit.

Step 3: The file page appears with the file name, size, and a Download button. There are no ads or other distractions.

Step 4: Click the Download button.

Step 5: Your browser will now decrypt the file. This may take a few seconds to a minute depending on file size and your device’s processing power. The download progress may not show immediately — wait patiently.

Step 6: Once decryption is complete, the download starts (or your browser asks where to save). Save the file to your Downloads folder or preferred location.

Step 7: After the download, locate the file and proceed with installation according to your edition (Bedrock or Java).

## 7. Troubleshooting Common Issues
Download not starting (seems stuck):
- The file is being decrypted in your browser. Wait. This delay is expected.
- For very large files (>1 GB), decryption can take several minutes on slower devices. Be patient.
- If nothing happens after 5 minutes, try refreshing the page once. Refreshing does not restart any progress because the file needs to be downloaded again anyway.
- Switch to a faster internet connection (Wi‑Fi) or use a different browser.

Slow download speeds:
- Proton Drive itself does not limit speeds, but your internet connection may be slow.
- Try downloading at a different time when your network is less congested.
- For very large files, consider using a desktop browser rather than a mobile device.

Browser compatibility issues:
- Proton Drive works best on modern browsers: Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Brave, and Safari.
- On iOS, Safari may sometimes have trouble with large decryptions. Use Chrome for iOS or the Proton Drive app as a fallback.
- Ensure JavaScript is enabled (required for client‑side decryption).

“Access Denied” or “Link Invalid”:
- The creator may have set an expiration date on the link, and it may have passed.
- The creator may have revoked access to the file.
- The link may have been typed incorrectly. Double‑check the URL.
- If the link is public but still gives access denied, ask the creator to verify the sharing settings.

Password prompt appears but no password was given:
- Contact the sharer. Proton Drive passwords are set by the file owner and are not sent automatically. You must obtain the password from the person who shared the link.

## 8. Mobile‑Specific Guidance
